About Silver Seraphim

I make photographs about being seen.

I believe everyone is carrying a kind of beauty they've never been shown. My whole practice is built to find it, hold it still, and hand it back.

Yes — I make beautiful images. But "pretty" was never the goal, and a flattering photo isn't the same as a true one. What I'm after is the moment a person stops managing how they look and simply lets themselves be present. That moment is unguarded, a little vulnerable, and unmistakably them. It's also where every photograph I care about was made.

I'm not photographing how you look. I'm photographing who you are when you finally let someone see.

That's why I work the way I do: slowly, collaboratively, and with consent at the center of everything. We talk before we ever shoot — about the concept, your comfort, what you want to say and what you'd rather not. We give the work room to be planned and done well, because rushing is the enemy of trust, and trust is the whole job.

Some of the most beautiful photographs I've ever made happened in the spaces between the poses — the second after someone exhaled and forgot the camera was there. My role is to build a room safe enough that those seconds arrive on their own, then be ready when they do.

If you've ever wanted to see yourself the way someone who really looked might see you — the emotion, the story, the quiet power you don't always notice in the mirror — that's what I do. Not pictures of you. Evidence of you.

Art deserves trust. So do the people creating it.
