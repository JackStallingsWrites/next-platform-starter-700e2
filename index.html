Meet me

I don't shoot pretty pictures. I shoot people.

Anyone can make a flattering image. What I'm after is the moment underneath it — the version of you that shows up when you stop performing and start trusting the room. That's where the real photograph lives.

I work slowly and collaboratively, because being seen takes a little time and a lot of safety. We talk first, we plan, and we don't rush. By the time you're in front of the camera, you already know you're in good hands.

You walk in wondering how it'll look. You walk out having been seen.
